Grapes

Organic grapes are a popular fruit that are grown without the use of synthetic fertilizers or pesticides. They are grown using natural farming methods that emphasize soil health and biodiversity, and are certified organic by regulatory bodies such as the USDA.


Organic grapes contain small amounts of other essential nutrients, including vitamin K, vitamin B6, and manganese.

organic grapes are a nutritious and healthy fruit that can be enjoyed as part of a balanced diet. They can be eaten on their own, added to salads or smoothies, or used in cooking and baking. When choosing organic grapes, it's important to select those that are firm, plump, and free from mold or wrinkles.

Nutrition Information

Resveratrol: Grapes contain resveratrol, a powerful antioxidant that has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and anti-cancer properties. Resveratrol is concentrated in the skin of grapes, which is why red and purple grapes are particularly high in this compound.

Vitamin C: Organic grapes are a good source of vitamin C, an antioxidant that helps to protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. One cup of grapes contains about 27% of the daily recommended intake of vitamin C.

Potassium: Grapes are also a good source of potassium, a mineral that helps to regulate blood pressure and support heart health. One cup of grapes contains about 8% of the daily recommended intake of potassium.
